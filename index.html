一
Pitch → Color

Each frequency range maps to a distinct tone. Deep bass becomes indigo and dark blue — grounding, heavy. Mid-range blooms into moss and ochre. High harmonics catch gold and rose, like light at the edge of a reed.

二
Volume → Form

Louder passages leave larger, more opaque marks. Quiet moments produce fine traces — the silence between brushstrokes. The resulting painting holds the full dynamic range of the song as texture and density.

三
Rhythm → Structure

Percussion and beat transients trigger bursts that scatter across the canvas. Fast rhythms fill the space densely; slow passages breathe. Over time, the painting accumulates the music's complete emotional arc.
